About Capybara

The capybara (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) is the largest living rodent in the world. Native to South America, capybaras are highly social, semi-aquatic animals often found near bodies of water like rivers, lakes, and marshes.

Features

Size: Adult capybaras weigh 35-66 kg (77-146 lbs) and are about 1-1.3 meters (3.2-4.2 feet) long. Appearance: They have barrel-shaped bodies, short heads, and reddish-brown fur on their upper body and yellowish-brown on their underside. Webbed feet: Capybaras have slightly webbed feet, which make them excellent swimmers. Diet: They are herbivores and primarily eat grasses, aquatic plants, and sometimes fruits and bark. Teeth: Their teeth grow continuously, similar to other rodents, and are worn down by constant grazing.
